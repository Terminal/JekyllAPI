All too common, a person will try to invite a bot,\nand they can’t see the server they are trying to invite.
\n

\n\n

If you see the following screen, and the server you want to invite a bot to isn’t available,\nyou can try to debug why this is not occurring, by following some simple steps.
\n

\n\n

Insight 1 - You are logged in to the wrong account

\n

You may be logged into Discord on the incorrect Discord account in your web browser.\nThis is common with users who have multiple Discord accounts,\nor has someone else logging into Discord using the web browser.

\n\n

How to fix

\n
    \n
  1. Go to https://discordapp.com/
    2. \n
  2. Click Open button, located in the top right hand corner of the screen
    \n
    3. \n
  3. After waiting for Discord to load in your web browser,\nclick on the cog on the bottom left hand corner of the screen.
    \n
    4. \n
  4. On the bottom left hand corner of the screen, click the Log Out button to log off.
    \n
    5. \n
  5. Click Log Out again to confirm your selection.
    6. \n
  6. Click Login, located in the top right hand corner of the screen
    \n
    7. \n
  7. Refresh the “Connect to Discord” page.
    8. \n
\n\n

If your servers are back, you can now invite your bot normally.\nIf not, make sure you are on the right account.\nTry alternative accounts that may be able to invite the bot.

\n\n

Insight 2 - You may not even have the right permissions

\n

Just because someone gave you the “Moderator” role in a server,\ndoesn’t mean you have permission to invite any bots.

\n\n

\n\n

How to fix

\n

To be able to invite bots, your server administrator must give you the Manage Server permission.\nPlease contact your administrator for this permission.

\n\n

If you are the administrator:

\n\n
    \n
  1. Find the name of the role that your administrators have.\nIn this example, administrators in the Terminal.ink server have the ohlookitsveld role.
    2. \n
  2. Click on the drop down arrow on the top left corner of the Discord window
    \n
    3. \n
  3. Click on Server Settings
    \n
    4. \n
  4. Click on the Roles tab
    \n
    5. \n
  5. Find the correct role, and toggle the Manage Server permission\n
    6. \n
  6. Save changes when you are finished\n
    7. \n
\n\n

Conclusion

\n

After following all steps, you should be able to invite a bot to your server.

\n\n

If, after following all steps, you fail to invite a bot,\nplease try and contact the technical support for the bot you are trying to invite,\nor join our Discord server for one on one help.
